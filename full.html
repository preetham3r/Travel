Raleigh Family Trip

March 20 – 22, 2026 · 3 Travelers

Before You Go

Pre-trip checklist for a smooth weekend

Book this week
  • Art in Bloom tickets — timed entry, popular weekend. Book at ncartmuseum.org
  • Fiction Kitchen Saturday dinner reservation — OpenTable or call (919) 999-0330
Weather intel
  • Friday: 68°F, sunny. Perfect for walking downtown.
  • Saturday: 78°F, 75% rain chance. Plan indoor activities (Art in Bloom, museums) for afternoon.
  • Sunday: 83°F, clearing. Nice for outdoor morning before driving home.
Packing notes
  • Rain jacket or umbrella (Saturday rain)
  • Comfortable walking shoes — downtown Raleigh is very walkable
  • Light layers — 68° to 83° range across the weekend
  • Camera gear if bringing the Canon
  • Snacks and water for the 4.5 hr drive
Drive plan
  • Route: Brambleton → I-66 W → I-81 S → I-85 S → Raleigh (~4.5 hrs)
  • Midpoint stop: South Hill or Henderson, VA (~2 hrs in)
  • Depart: 8:30 AM Friday → arrive ~1:00 PM
  • Tolls: Minimal. ~$5 on I-85
  • Gas: Fill up before departure. ~$55 each way.

Drive + Settle In · 68°F Sunny

Should 🚶 Downtown Raleigh stroll
Walk Fayetteville Street — the main downtown pedestrian corridor. State Capitol grounds, Moore Square, browse shops on Hargett Street. Easy, low key first afternoon. The Sip n' Stroll social district lets you walk downtown with a drink from any participating bar.
📍 Fayetteville St

Casual browse. Multiple vendors, good coffee, local vibe. Nice spot for an afternoon snack or drink.
📍 Transfer Co.

Laotian restaurant near Moore Square. Excellent vegetarian options: basil stir fry with tofu, papaya salad, spring rolls. Great cocktails. Adi will enjoy the flavors.
222 S. Blount St.
📍 Directions
Day 1 notes
  • Easy pace. Long drive, so keep the afternoon light.
  • Downtown is very walkable from any parking garage on Fayetteville or Wilmington St.
  • If Bida Manda has a wait, backup: Sosta Cafe (130 E. Davie St) for sandwiches, or Transfer Co. Food Hall vendors.
  • Budget estimate: Gas ~$55, dinner ~$80, parking ~$10. Total ~$145.

Art in Bloom + Downtown · 78°F · 75% Rain

Annual five-day event pairing stunning artworks with creative floral arrangements. This year's theme: "Written in the Stars." Massive floral installations throughout the galleries. Tickets ~$25/person.
Perfect rainy afternoon activity. The 164-acre museum park and sculpture garden are worth a walk if rain holds off.
2110 Blue Ridge Rd. (~15 min drive from downtown)
📍 Directions
4:30 PM
Nice 🌳 Museum Park walk

5:30 PM
🏠 Rest break
Back to host's place. Recharge before dinner.
7:00 PM
Must 🍽️ Dinner — Fiction Kitchen
100% vegetarian restaurant. Creative comfort food: curry bowls with coconut curry and basmati, apple and beet chaat, mac + cheese, "chicken" and waffles. Strong cocktail program — try the Eastern NC Bloody Mary or Cherish the Beet. Adi friendly.
🌿 All Veg
2409 Crabtree Blvd., Ste. 100

Day 2 notes
  • This is the big day. Two anchors: Museum of Natural Sciences (free, morning) and Art in Bloom (ticketed, afternoon).
  • Art in Bloom is 15 min drive from downtown. Use your car.
  • Fiction Kitchen gets busy Saturday night. Reservation strongly recommended.
  • Budget estimate: Art in Bloom ~$75, meals ~$100, parking ~$15, misc ~$25. Total ~$215.
